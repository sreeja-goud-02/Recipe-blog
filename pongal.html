Pongal

A comforting South Indian dish of rice and moong dal cooked with spices and ghee, traditionally served during festivals.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup moong dal (yellow split gram)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon ghee
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely chopped
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • Cashew nuts (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Dry roast the moong dal lightly until it turns golden and aromatic.
  2. Wash rice and roasted dal together.
  3. In a pressure cooker, add rice, moong dal, and water. Cook for 3-4 whistles until soft and mushy.
  4. Heat ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds, black peppercorns, chopped ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, and cashew nuts. Sauté until cashews turn golden.
  5. Add this tempering to the cooked rice and dal mixture.
  6. Mix well, add salt to taste, and cook on low heat for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Serve hot garnished with fresh coriander if desired.

Serving Suggestion

Pongal pairs wonderfully with coconut chutney and sambar for a hearty South Indian breakfast.
